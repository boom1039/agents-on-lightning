# Money

Use this workflow for wallet funds, capital funds, paid analytics, and paid help.

## What Money Means

The wallet is agent spending money.
Capital is the agent's deployable channel funding.
Analytics and help are paid services.
The app takes no platform fees and no commissions.
Tools may still surface real Lightning invoices, network costs, service prices, or withdrawal costs.
Keep these states separate when deciding what an agent can do next.

## Wallet

1. `aol_get_wallet_balance`
2. `aol_get_wallet_history`
3. `aol_get_wallet_mint_quote_help`
4. `aol_create_wallet_mint_quote`
5. `aol_check_wallet_mint_quote`
6. `aol_mint_wallet`
7. `aol_create_wallet_melt_quote`
8. `aol_melt_wallet`
9. `aol_send_wallet_tokens`
10. `aol_receive_wallet_tokens`
11. `aol_restore_wallet`
12. `aol_reclaim_wallet_pending`
13. `aol_decode_invoice`

Create a mint quote, pay the returned invoice externally, check the quote, then mint only after payment is confirmed.
Unpaid invoices are normal blockers, not failures to hide.
Use `aol_decode_invoice` before acting on a Lightning invoice when invoice meaning, amount, or expiry matters.
Wallet history is the audit trail for agent spending money.

## Capital

1. `aol_get_capital_balance`
2. `aol_get_capital_activity`
3. `aol_create_onchain_capital_deposit`
4. `aol_create_lightning_capital_deposit`
5. `aol_get_lightning_capital_deposit_status`
6. `aol_retry_lightning_capital_deposit`
7. `aol_get_capital_deposits`
8. `aol_withdraw_capital`

Use capital for channel operations.
On-chain deposits can take one or more blocks to confirm, and exact timing is not guaranteed.
Lightning capital deposits may pass through a bridge provider or wallet fallback and can take roughly 20-40+ minutes before capital becomes available.
Retry Lightning capital flows only when status says retry is allowed.
Do not treat invoice paid, bridge pending, confirmation pending, fallback pending, or locked funds as spendable capital.
If the human asks you to open a channel and available capital is too low, ask how they want to fund channel capital before continuing the open flow.

## Channel Funding Choices

1. `aol_create_onchain_capital_deposit`: use when the payer can send on-chain bitcoin and can wait for confirmations.
2. `aol_create_lightning_capital_deposit`: use when the payer wants to pay a Lightning invoice into channel capital; this is not wallet ecash, not wallet spending money, and not a direct on-chain funding address.
3. `aol_fund_channel_from_ecash`: use only when wallet ecash already exists and a signed channel-funding instruction is ready.
4. `aol_create_wallet_mint_quote`: use for wallet ecash spending money, not direct channel capital.
5. Channel-open mining-fee policy comes from live market config and proof/cost results; do not assume operator subsidy.

## Revenue Context

Routing-fee revenue belongs to the market workflow.
Review it with `aol_get_market_revenue`, `aol_get_market_revenue_channel`, `aol_get_market_performance`, and `aol_get_market_performance_channel`.
Do not infer routing income from wallet or capital balances alone.

## Analytics And Help

1. `aol_get_analytics_catalog`
2. `aol_quote_analytics`
3. `aol_execute_analytics`
4. `aol_get_analytics_history`
5. `aol_request_help`

Quote before execute.
Use paid help only when wallet or capital can pay.
Do not paste private keys, bearer tokens, credentials, or seed material into paid help.
Quotes let agents know the cost before spending.
History prevents repeated paid work when a prior answer already exists.

## Stop Conditions

1. Stop if balance is zero and a tool requires payment.
2. Stop if an invoice is unpaid.
3. Stop if a deposit is still waiting for confirmations, bridge completion, or fallback completion.
4. Stop if a withdrawal destination is invalid.
