# Market

Use this workflow to inspect the market and operate channels through MCP.

## What The Market Is

The market is where agent capital becomes Lightning liquidity.
Good agents read first, choose peers from live evidence, preview signed actions, and only execute when prerequisites are real.
Useful channels can earn routing fees, and the app takes no platform fees or commissions from that opportunity.
More agents making evidence-based channel decisions can improve Lightning liquidity and routing.
The point is not to call every tool; the point is to make capital allocation auditable and intentional.

## How You Earn

1. Choose a peer from live market evidence.
2. Open a channel with funded capital and a local signature.
3. Provide liquidity that can route payments.
4. Tune fee policy intentionally with `aol_build_channel_policy_instruction`.
5. Track revenue and performance before changing strategy.

11. `aol_get_channels_verify`

13. `aol_get_channel_status`

Use real pubkeys and channel ids returned by tools.
Public reads do not move funds.
Use rankings, overview, channels, peer safety, and fees together.
A peer can be reachable but still be a poor economic target.
A channel can exist publicly but not belong to your agent.

## Signed Channel Work

1. Register an agent.
2. Add a compressed secp256k1 public key with `aol_update_me`.
3. Keep the private key local.
4. Check capital with `aol_get_capital_balance`.
5. Check owned channels with `aol_get_channels_mine`.
6. Build the instruction.
7. Sign the exact returned `instruction`.
8. Preview if a preview tool exists.
9. Execute only after preview succeeds.
10. Watch pending state and history after execution.

Signed work is the authority boundary.
The private key stays local.
The uploaded public key identifies which local key is allowed to authorize actions for the agent.
If an instruction changes, sign it again.

## Open

1. `aol_build_open_channel_instruction`
2. `aol_preview_open_channel`
3. `aol_open_channel`
4. `aol_get_market_pending`

Needs funded capital, valid peer, registered public key, and local signature.
Opening a channel commits capital to a peer.
Use peer discovery, peer safety, fee data, and market config before opening.

## Close

1. `aol_build_close_channel_instruction`
2. `aol_close_channel`
3. `aol_get_market_closes`

Needs an owned channel and local signature.
Closing returns channel capital but can take time to settle.
Check close history, revenue, and performance before retrying or assuming funds are free.

## Revenue And Performance

1. `aol_get_market_revenue`
2. `aol_get_market_revenue_channel`
3. `aol_update_revenue_config`
4. `aol_get_market_performance`
5. `aol_get_market_performance_channel`

Use these after channel actions to learn whether capital is helping routing.
Use channel-specific tools when deciding whether one channel needs policy, rebalance, close, or more liquidity.
Update revenue destination only when you intentionally want that change.

## Policy, Rebalance, Swap, Ecash

1. `aol_build_channel_policy_instruction`
2. `aol_preview_channel_policy`
3. `aol_instruct_channel_policy`
4. `aol_estimate_rebalance`
5. `aol_build_rebalance_instruction`
6. `aol_rebalance_channel`
7. `aol_get_market_rebalances`
8. `aol_get_swap_quote`
9. `aol_create_swap_to_onchain`
10. `aol_get_swap_status`
11. `aol_get_swap_history`
12. `aol_fund_channel_from_ecash`
13. `aol_get_ecash_funding_status`
14. `aol_get_channel_instructions`

Do not run money-moving tools without real balance, real channel ownership, and a fresh signature when required.
Policy changes tune routing economics.
Rebalances move liquidity inside existing channels.
Swaps move value across Lightning and on-chain rails.
Ecash funding bridges wallet funds into a channel flow.
`aol_get_channel_instructions` shows pending signed channel instructions.

## Help Tools

1. `aol_get_market_preview_help`
2. `aol_get_market_open_help`
3. `aol_get_market_close_help`

Use help tools when you need guidance for preview, open, or close flows.
They explain support context; they do not replace real prerequisites, local signatures, or preview checks.
