LIGHTNING NETWORK BASICS:

Channels: Bidirectional payment channels between two Lightning nodes. Each channel has a capacity (total sats locked in the 2-of-2 multisig), local balance (your side), and remote balance (peer's side). Channel points are formatted as "txid:output_index".

Routing: Payments traverse multiple channels via onion routing. Each hop charges a fee (base_fee_msat + fee_rate_ppm * amount / 1000000). Routing nodes earn fees by forwarding payments.

Fees: Two components — base_fee_msat (flat per-forward, in millisatoshis) and fee_rate_ppm (proportional, parts per million of forwarded amount). Example: base_fee=1000 msat + fee_rate=100 ppm means forwarding 100,000 sats costs 1 sat base + 10 sats proportional = 11 sats.

Liquidity: Local balance = sats you can send outward. Remote balance = sats that can flow inward to you. A well-balanced channel (50/50) can route in both directions. Channels deplete toward one side over time from routing.

Rebalancing: Moving sats from channels with excess local balance to channels with depleted local balance. Done via circular payments through the network. Costs routing fees.

HOW TO READ AUDIT LOG ENTRIES:

Each audit entry has these fields:
- type: The event type (channel_assigned, fee_instruction_executed, fee_instruction_rejected, violation_detected, checkpoint, etc.)
- _ts: Unix timestamp in milliseconds
- hash: SHA-256 hash of the entry (for tamper detection)
- prev_hash: Hash of the previous entry (chain linkage)
- agent_id: Which agent triggered this event
- chan_id: The channel this event relates to (uint64 decimal string)
- channel_point: The channel's funding transaction ("txid:output_index")

Common entry types:
- channel_assigned: An agent was assigned control of a channel. Check constraints field for fee bounds.
- fee_instruction_executed: A fee change was successfully applied. Look at old_policy and new_policy for before/after.
- fee_instruction_rejected: A fee change was rejected. The reason field explains why (signature invalid, out of bounds, cooldown active, etc.).
- violation_detected: The monitor detected fees outside the allowed range. Includes detected_policy and allowed_range.
- checkpoint: Periodic integrity checkpoint. entry_count shows total chain length.

HOW TO INTERPRET CHANNEL METRICS:

- capacity: Total sats in the channel. Larger = more routing potential but higher capital commitment.
- local_balance / remote_balance: Current liquidity split. Aim for balance ratios that match your strategy.
- total_satoshis_sent / total_satoshis_received: Lifetime flow. High asymmetry suggests one-directional demand.
- num_updates: How many state updates (payments routed). Higher = more active channel.
- uptime: Percentage of time the remote peer is online. Below 95% is concerning.
- fee_rate_ppm: Your current fee rate. Compare against network median (~50-200 ppm for most channels).
- base_fee_msat: Your current base fee. Most nodes use 0-1000 msat.

COMMON FAILURE MODES:

1. "Signature invalid" — You signed the wrong data. Instructions must be signed as canonical JSON (RFC 8785): keys sorted alphabetically, no whitespace. Use the preview endpoint first to verify.

2. "Channel not assigned to this agent" — You're trying to modify a channel you don't control. Check GET /api/v1/channels/mine for your assignments.

3. "Fee outside allowed range" — Your instruction violates the constraints set when the channel was assigned. Check the constraints field in your assignment.

4. "Cooldown active" — You changed fees too recently. Each channel has a cooldown period between fee changes.

5. "Insufficient ecash balance" — Your wallet doesn't have enough sats. Fund it via POST /api/v1/wallet/mint-quote flow.
